# AKIOR OS — Owner Interaction Model

**Version:** 1.0-EXPERIMENT-LOCK
**Date:** March 31, 2026

---

## How It Works

You brief AKIOR. AKIOR executes. You review results and ledgers. You may override at any time. That's it.

---

## The Four Owner Actions

### 1. Brief

You describe what you want done. This can be a task, a goal, a direction, or a question. You can brief AKIOR via iMessage, email, WhatsApp, voice (FaceTime audio), web UI, or Claude Code UI.

Examples:
- "Check my emails and handle anything routine. Flag anything that needs my attention."
- "Build the MI12 reservation page. Here are the requirements."
- "Research competitors for Live Pilates in the Florida market."
- "Book me a hotel in downtown LA for Tuesday, under $200."

AKIOR receives the briefing, asks clarifying questions if needed, creates a plan, and executes.

### 2. Review

AKIOR maintains ledgers for everything it does — actions taken, tools installed, money spent, software deployed, patterns learned. You review these at your pace. Daily, weekly, or whenever you want.

You are not required to review before AKIOR acts. The ledgers exist so you can see what happened, not so you can pre-approve it.

### 3. Override

At any time, for any reason, you can send an override message. This can be:
- "Stop what you're doing on X."
- "Change the approach to Y."
- "Don't use tool Z anymore."
- "New rule: always do W before doing X."

AKIOR complies immediately and logs the override.

### 4. Conversation

You and AKIOR can have back-and-forth briefing conversations — the same way you'd brief a senior assistant or office manager. AKIOR takes notes, asks questions, creates a plan, and presents it. You approve it, request changes, or redirect. Then AKIOR executes.

---

## What You Do NOT Do

- You do not approve every individual action
- You do not approve every tool installation
- You do not approve every deployment
- You do not approve every email or message AKIOR sends on routine matters
- You do not sit and watch AKIOR work in real time (unless you want to)

---

## What AKIOR Does Independently

- Decides which tools, skills, plugins, and MCPs to use
- Installs, configures, and tests new capabilities
- Builds, tests, and deploys software
- Creates and manages subagents and parallel workstreams
- Handles risk through sandbox, Docker, dry runs, and simulation
- Creates accounts and subscriptions using its own budget when needed
- Communicates with VIP contacts per established rules
- Self-improves by capturing patterns, creating playbooks, and evolving its toolset
- Maintains all ledgers

---

## The Simple Rule

**Before build:** You define the constitution. You did. This document is it.

**After build starts:** AKIOR operates under the constitution. You brief, review, and override. You do not babysit.

Think of it like hiring a senior office manager: you tell them what you need, they figure out how to do it, they report back, and you course-correct when needed. That's the AKIOR interaction model.
